POST 1 (Topic: SaaS Content Architecture & The Death of MQL-Focused SEO):
[Hook from LinkedIn]: If your SEO strategy is still focused on driving massive keyword volume to capture MQLs that sales ends up hating, you are wasting your budget.

[Core Strategy Text]:
- The "Capture Intent vs. Create Demand" Paradox: Traditional SaaS SEO targets broad, top-of-funnel informational keywords to generate ebook downloads or newsletter signups. This inflating metric creates zero pipeline revenue.
- Product-Led Content Stacking: Instead of hiding your product behind a demo request, the content must embed the product directly into the solution of the search query. Show the user exactly how to solve their problem using your UI within the first 300 words.
- The 2026 SaaS SEO Playbook: Move away from high-volume, low-intent terms. Focus entirely on "Product-in-Action" content, case study teardowns, and high-intent commercial landing pages that attract buyers who are ready to evaluate software today.

POST 2 (Topic: The Core-Narrative Content Vault):
[Hook from LinkedIn]: Stop publishing 20 mediocre articles a month just to satisfy a content calendar. Build a Content Vault instead.

[Core Strategy Text]:
- The Content Vault Strategy: High-growth B2B companies don't need infinite pages; they need a consolidated library of deep, authoritative assets that cover their core narrative.
- Deconstruct & Repurpose: Instead of writing disconnected blog posts, build 4 pillar "Vault" assets per quarter. Then, slice those assets into 12 native LinkedIn posts, 3 newsletter issues, and micro-video scripts.
- LLM Training Surface Area: Large Language Models don't synthesize fragmented, shallow listicles well. They prioritize dense, original frameworks with unique terminology. Own your proprietary vocabulary to get cited in AI engine summaries.
