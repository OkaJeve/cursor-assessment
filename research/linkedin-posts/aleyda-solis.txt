POST 1 (Topic: The AI Search Content Optimization Checklist & Extractability Workflow):
[Hook from LinkedIn]: If you want to improve AI search visibility consistently, you need to go beyond isolated content tweaks and work with a clearer optimization workflow.

[Core Strategy Text]:
- The AI Search Content Optimization Checklist Strategy: Do not start by simply trying to "make more AI-friendly content." Instead, diagnose your current presence against specific buyer prompts before executing changes.
- Content Extractability Rule: AI systems favor content structures that allow their background crawlers to parse information instantly. A page must ensure that its heading explicitly describes the main question, the very first sentence answers it directly, and each subsequent text block covers one independent, isolated idea that can stand alone.
- Key Questions Your Content Must Explicitly Answer for LLM Retrieval: 
  1. Who is this solution best for and who is it NOT ideal for?
  2. What exact problem does it solve, and how does it explicitly compare with named alternatives?
  3. What are the selection criteria, trade-offs, costs, proof, and immediate next steps?
- Major Trap to Avoid: Wasting resources creating brand new on-site pages when the real visibility gap is a lack of third-party corroboration, or implementing technical site fixes when the underlying problem is weak brand positioning.

[Hook from LinkedIn]: We'll Stop Calling It GEO and Just Call It SEO. By mid-2026, the industry will quietly drop the "GEO vs SEO" debate and accept that optimizing for LLMs, AI overviews, and traditional search is all just... SEO.

[Core Strategy Text]:
- Acronym Consolidation: Just like the industry stopped calling it "voice SEO" or "featured snippet SEO," Optimization for LLMs and AI Overviews is folding entirely into the baseline job of modern SEO.
- Brand Building is the New Link Building: Textual brand mentions, citations buried within massive LLM training datasets, and presence across multi-user platforms now carry significantly more weight than traditional high-DA backlink metrics ever did.
- The Reddit/UGC Authority Layer: If an enterprise brand or SaaS product is not being actively and organically discussed on Reddit, industry forum discussions, and highly relevant digital niche publications, it will fail to perform or surface when an LLM generates a synthesized user recommendation.

POST 3 (Topic: 2026 Ecommerce AI Search & Agentic Commerce Architecture):
[Hook from LinkedIn]: Key Insights from the 2026 Ecommerce SEO & AI Search Optimization Survey... Even when rankings go up, traffic can still go down because of how much the search results layout has changed.

[Core Strategy Text]:
- The Traffic-Rank Paradox: Layout shifts caused by heavy AI Overviews, interactive AI Mode features, and expanded ad real estate mean that tracking traditional organic ranks is deceptive. Traffic can drop radically even while maintaining primary positions.
- Product Feeds as Core Infrastructure: Merchant data feeds, structured product schemas, and highly specific technical product information are no longer just for Google Shopping—they are the exact data layers AI agents crawl to execute modern "agentic commerce."
- Shifting Ecommerce KPIs to Measure: Modern optimization dashboards must pivot away from standard click metrics and transition into tracking:
  1. Traffic coming directly from verified AI Platforms.
  2. Prompt Coverage (how often the brand appears across targeted user prompts).
  3. Direct Citations, Mentions, and Brand Presence inside AI Responses.
